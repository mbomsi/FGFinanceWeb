Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Everything you need to know about our operations, membership, and regulatory framework as a Category 1 Credit Union.

Welcome to our FAQ section. Here, you'll find comprehensive answers about how a Category 1 Credit Union operates, our services, member responsibilities, and the regulatory environment under OHADA and COBAC/MINFI.

Category 1 Credit Union Essentials

1. What is a Category 1 Credit Union (CAT 1)?

A Category 1 Credit Union is a **member-owned financial cooperative** that mobilizes savings and provides loans **only to its members**. It operates under **COBAC Regulation R-2002/01** and is supervised by the Ministry of Finance (MINFI).

2. Who can become a member of a Credit Union?

Any person who: **is of legal working age (18+), shares the bond of association** (community, profession, etc.), and **accepts the by-laws**. Membership requires filling a form and paying the registration fee and minimum share capital.

3. What are the main objectives of a CAT 1 Credit Union?

To **encourage members to save regularly**, **provide affordable loans**, promote financial literacy, and ensure members’ financial inclusion and empowerment.

4. What services does a Category 1 Credit Union provide?

Services include **Savings accounts** (ordinary, fixed, special), **Loan facilities** (short, medium, long-term, emergency), member education programs, and micro-insurance linkages.

5. Can non-members deposit or take loans from a CAT 1 Credit Union?

❌ **No**. By regulation, a Category 1 Credit Union deals strictly with its **members only**. Non-members cannot hold accounts or benefit from loans.

6. What are the eligibility requirements for obtaining a loan?

A member must: be registered for at least **three months** (usually), have a **regular savings record**, maintain a **positive account balance**, provide member **guarantors** in good standing, and demonstrate **repayment capacity**.

7. What is the difference between savings and shares?

**Savings** are amounts members can generally withdraw. **Shares** represent a member’s permanent ownership contribution, are non-withdrawable, except upon leaving the Credit Union.

8. How is interest determined on loans and savings?

**Interest on Loans** is set by the Board (typically 1–2.5% per month). **Interest on Savings** (dividends) is declared annually after financial results are approved at the Annual General Meeting (AGM).

9. How does the Credit Union ensure the safety of members’ money?

Through management by elected committees, **regular internal and external audits**, adherence to **COBAC prudential norms**, cash deposits in approved banks, and **Insurance coverage** (e.g., Loan Protection and Savings Insurance - LPSI).

10. What are the main governing organs of a Credit Union?

The **General Assembly (GA)** is the supreme body. Others include the **Board of Directors (BOD)** for strategy, the **Supervisory Committee** for internal audit, the **Credit Committee** for loan approval, and the **Management Staff** for day-to-day operations.

11. How are Credit Union leaders chosen?

All leaders (Board, Supervisory, and Credit Committees) are **elected by members** during the Annual General Meeting (AGM) based on merit, trust, and compliance with the by-laws.

12. What are the responsibilities of members?

Members must **save regularly**, **repay loans promptly**, attend meetings and participate in elections, respect the rules, and promote mutual assistance.

13. What happens when a member defaults on a loan?

The Credit Union may deduct funds from the member’s savings/shares. **Guarantors** may be required to pay. Legal recovery measures may follow for persistent defaults, affecting the member's credit reputation.

14. Can a member withdraw from the Credit Union?

✅ **Yes**. A member can resign by submitting a written notice. The Credit Union will refund the member’s savings and shares (after offsetting any outstanding obligations).

15. How does a Credit Union generate income?

Main sources include **Loan interest** from members, registration and transaction fees, fines, penalties, and **Investment income** (e.g., fixed deposits).

16. How are profits (surpluses) shared?

After the annual audit, surpluses are distributed as: **Reserve fund** (minimum 20%), **member dividends on shares**, interest on savings, and contributions to education/social funds.

17. What is the role of MINFI and COBAC in Credit Union supervision?

**MINFI** (Ministry of Finance) oversees national regulation and licensing. **COBAC** ensures prudential supervision and financial stability of microfinance institutions across CEMAC countries.

18. How often are Credit Union accounts audited?

**Internally**: At least once every quarter by the Supervisory Committee. **Externally**: Annually, by a certified CAMCCUL or MINFI-approved auditor.

19. What is the difference between Category 1 and Category 2 institutions?

**Category 1 (CAT 1)**: Deals only with members, operating as a cooperative society. **Category 2 (CAT 2)**: Deals with members and the general public (e.g., microfinance institutions).

20. How can a Credit Union grow its membership and capital base?

By organizing financial literacy programs, building trust, offering attractive loan products, establishing community partnerships, and encouraging youth/women’s participation.

21. What happens if a member dies while owing a loan?

Most Credit Unions are covered by **Loan Protection and Savings Insurance (LPSI)**, which typically **cancels outstanding loans** upon the member’s death and pays a benefit to the designated beneficiary.

22. How are disputes resolved in the Credit Union?

Minor disputes are settled internally by the Board or Supervisory Committee. Unresolved cases are referred to the Union’s League Office (CAMCCUL) or the local cooperative registrar. Legal disputes may go to Cooperative Tribunals.

23. What are some best practices for members?

Keep contact details updated, **attend AGMs and participate actively**, encourage others to join, report suspicious activities, and maintain a **strong savings discipline**.

24. What happens if the Credit Union faces financial difficulties?

The Board notifies CAMCCUL and MINFI. A recovery plan is initiated, potentially involving audits, mergers, or restructuring. Members are kept informed.

25. How does the Credit Union contribute to community development?

Through its Education Fund and Social Fund, the Credit Union may support members’ children with **scholarships**, fund community health programs, and sponsor economic empowerment initiatives.
